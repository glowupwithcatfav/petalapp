📚 Good to know

Myths vs facts

The vagina cleans itself, you only need to wash the outer area (vulva) with warm water. Douching and scented washes disrupt your natural balance more than they help. If it smells strongly different or itches, that is your body asking for a check up, not a product.

When to see a doctor

New strong odor, unusual discharge, burning when you pee, ongoing itching, or pain during sex are all reasons to book a check, not to self diagnose. Your body is not broken, it is just telling you something.

Safe product use

Fragrance free, pH balanced products are gentler on sensitive skin. Change pads or tampons every 4 to 6 hours. Cotton underwear breathes better than synthetic fabric, especially overnight.

📖 The four phases

Menstrual (days 1–5)

Energy is lower, cramps are common. Gentle intimate care, warmth, and extra iron rich food help.

Follicular (days 6–13)

Energy rises, skin often clears up. Good window for new routines and workouts.

Ovulation (days 14–16)

Discharge increases, this is normal. Skin may glow, confidence often peaks here.

Luteal (days 17–28)

Oil production rises, breakouts and mood dips are common. Extra rest and gentle skincare help.

📖 Lesson: skin & hygiene basics that actually matter

Sun protection by skin tone

Ingrown hairs & razor bumps

Exfoliate 24 hours before hair removal, always use a fresh blade, and moisturize right after. Shaving in the direction of hair growth reduces bumps significantly.

Sweat & odor management

Clean and dry skin folds fully after workouts. Breathable cotton fabric worn close to skin reduces bacteria buildup that causes odor.

Common skin concerns

Keloid-prone skin should treat cuts and piercings extra gently. Eczema-prone skin benefits from fragrance-free, thick moisturizers applied right after showering while skin is damp.

Life Stages

Your needs change, this section grows with you.

Teens & first period

Focus on the basics: front to back wiping, changing pads every 4–6 hours, breathable cotton underwear. Periods can be irregular for the first couple years, that is normal.

20s & 30s

Sexual activity, hormonal birth control, and stress can all shift discharge, cycle length, and skin. Regular check ups matter even when nothing feels wrong.

Pregnancy

Increased discharge is common and normal. Avoid douching and scented products more than ever, skin sensitivity often increases too.

Perimenopause & menopause

Dryness, thinner skin, and irregular cycles are common as estrogen drops. Gentle, fragrance-free intimate moisturizers can help a lot here.
